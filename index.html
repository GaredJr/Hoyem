A worldbuilding archive built to hold lore, controls, characters, and places without making the whole thing feel like a lifeless database.

  • Searchable archive with grouped entries and direct links.
  • Reading layout built for long-form notes, not cramped cards.

Mini case study

Turning the archive into a better product

This pass was about making the wiki easier to trust and easier to use: less shell behavior, better navigation, and a smoother experience once you land deep in an entry.

  • Static entry pages replaced JS-only rendering for stronger linking and resilience.
  • Search and category filters now restore from the URL and reset cleanly.
  • Section navigation now helps readers move through longer entries without losing their place.

Hoyem Framework

A reusable styling layer extracted from the portfolio and Omni Wiki, built so new pages can inherit the same atmosphere without rebuilding the whole design language from scratch.

  • Shared dark and light theme tokens pulled from the existing site.
  • hf- prefixed primitives for layout, cards, controls, and navigation chrome.

World Exploration

Map & Locations

Places worth remembering, plus the routes and landmarks that shape the journey.

A loose map of the places that already feel important, even if some names and borders are still likely to change.

  • Good location notes should tell you what a place feels like, not just what is there.
  • Routes matter because tension often comes from the journey, not only the destination.
  • The best landmarks do double duty: they help you navigate and they carry story weight.

Outer Rim

The Outer Rim feels like the edge of familiar ground. It is a good place to teach scavenging, route-reading, and that low-grade sense that something is slightly off.

It should feel open enough to learn in, but never fully comfortable.

  • Good first-stop region for traversal basics and manageable danger.
  • Plenty of room for small discoveries and environmental hints.
  • A natural place for the first warnings that the world is changing.

Flooded Pass

Flooded Pass immediately suggests bad weather, blocked routes, and the need to slow down. Even as a rough idea, it already carries friction.

It feels like the kind of place players remember because they had to work to get through it.

  • Potential hazard: unstable footing or a shifting waterline.
  • Potential reward: a hidden route back toward camp.
  • Potential story beat: the first obvious sign that the region is changing.

Observation Tower

The Observation Tower feels like a reveal point. It is the kind of place where the player finally sees how the world fits together, or how much worse it has gotten.

That makes it useful both as a navigational reward and as a turning point in the story.

  • Good place for map updates or journal discoveries.
  • Could support binocular, radio, or beacon mechanics.
  • Works for both exploration payoffs and bigger narrative turns.

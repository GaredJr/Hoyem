Storyline

The current story spine, from opening setup to bigger reveals.

A rough story outline meant to hold tone, pacing, and momentum before every scene is locked in.

  • The opening should make the group feel real before the bigger problem fully shows itself.
  • Midgame tension works best when familiar places come back changed.
  • The late reveal should answer the mystery without draining all the intrigue out of it.

Opening setup

The story starts close to familiar ground, where a routine objective begins to feel wrong in small ways.

That opening stretch should introduce the cast, set the tone, and hint at the mystery without rushing to explain it.

Midgame pressure

The middle should send the player back through places they thought they understood, only now with more risk, less certainty, and more at stake.

  • Camp upgrades open up new tactics and new conversations.
  • Earlier allies start showing conflicting goals.
  • Travel routes become more dangerous or, sometimes, newly open.

Late reveal

The late reveal should make the earlier clues snap into focus, then force a choice that matters to the group as much as it matters to the world around them.

  • Make clear what the group misunderstood early on.
  • Resolve at least one relationship arc before the ending lands.
  • Leave some room for aftermath, fallout, or future notes.

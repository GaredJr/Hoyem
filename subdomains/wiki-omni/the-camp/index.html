The Camp

Notes on the home base, its routines, and why it matters between expeditions.

The camp is where the world finally slows down. It is part refuge, part workshop, part social space, and it should feel like more than a menu with a fire pit.

  • A good camp should reflect what happened outside it.
  • The hub works best when practical upgrades and story downtime live side by side.
  • Players return for safety, but they remember the camp because of the people and the small changes.

Why it matters

The camp resets the rhythm between expeditions, but it should also be the place where consequences finally catch up with the player.

It gives the group room to plan, recover, and notice what changed after the last trip out.

  • A safe place for dialogue, downtime, and smaller character moments.
  • The main space for upgrades and progression tracking.
  • A natural place to quietly restate what matters before the next outing.

Stations

Breaking the camp into stations keeps it readable without flattening it. Each corner should hint at a habit, a person, or a routine.

  • Workshop for repair, tuning, and the practical side of staying alive.
  • Planning table for map review, route choices, and mission selection.
  • Fire pit or commons area for quieter conversations and morale beats.

How it changes

A camp starts to feel real when it changes with the story. New tools, repaired structures, and fresh ambient dialogue make progress feel lived in instead of logged.

  • Let story milestones leave visible marks on the space.
  • Unlock companion interactions through trust or quest completion.
  • Make new camp tools feed back into field exploration.

Characters

Early notes on the cast, their roles, and how they play off each other.

These are early character notes, not locked biographies. The goal is to catch what each person feels like, what role they seem to fill in the group, and where their story might start to shift.

  • Ali and Amy still feel like the clearest anchors in the current material.
  • The focus here is tone, chemistry, and first read, not final canon.
  • If the cast expands, each character can branch into a full entry of their own.

Ali

Ali comes across as the grounded one in the group: practical, observant, and easy to trust early.

The gear and silhouette do a lot of quiet work here. Ali feels built for rough conditions, which makes them a strong anchor once the world starts getting stranger.

  • Visual hook: practical gear and a solid silhouette.
  • Narrative role: guide, scout, or first companion.
  • Emotional read: steady enough to ground riskier personalities.

Amy

Amy brings a different energy. Where Ali feels steady, Amy feels sharper and more analytical, like someone who notices the extra layer in a situation before anyone says it out loud.

That difference gives the pair immediate shape. You can imagine it surfacing in dialogue, priorities, and how they read the same problem.

  • Visual hook: a portrait that reads differently from Ali even at thumbnail size.
  • Narrative role: strategist, support, or emotional counterweight.
  • Dialogue texture: quicker, sharper, and a little harder to read.

Relationship notes

The relationships matter because the cast should feel connected, not filed away as separate profile cards. The more camp scenes, field choices, and shared pressure change how people read each other, the more useful this page becomes.

  • Track the big trust shifts after each turning point.
  • Note which camp upgrades unlock new conversations.
  • Keep heavier spoilers deeper in the entry once the final story order is set.
